"How much does an MVP cost?" is one of the first questions founders ask—and one of the hardest to answer directly.

The truth is, MVP costs range from **$2,000 to $150,000+** depending on your approach, features, and team. This guide breaks down every cost component so you can budget accurately.

## The Three MVP Phases

At FirstMileDev, we break MVP development into three phases. Most startups should start with Phase 1:

| Phase | Cost | Time | Core Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Validation** | $2,000 | 2-4 weeks | Landing page, Ad campaign, Waitlist signup |
| **Phase 2: MVP Build** | $5,000 | 4-12 weeks | Core features, User auth, Basic dashboard |
| **Phase 3: Scale** | Custom | Long-term | Advanced features, Performance, Security compliance |

## Hidden Costs Most Founders Forget

These costs don't show up in development quotes but can derail your budget:

- **Hosting & Infrastructure ($50-500/month)**: AWS, Heroku, Vercel costs add up quickly at scale. MVP hosting starts at $50/month but can hit $500+ with traffic.
- **Software Subscriptions ($50-300/month)**: Email (Mailchimp/SendGrid), analytics (Mixpanel), payments (Stripe), SMS (Twilio)—costs accumulate.
- **Post-Launch Support ($500-2,000/month)**: Bugs, feature requests, security updates. Budget 20% of development cost annually for maintenance.
- **Legal & Compliance ($500-5,000+)**: Terms of service, privacy policy, possibly HIPAA/SOC2 for regulated industries.

## How to Budget Wisely

Before you start spending, follow this checklist:

- **Validate before building**: Spend $2,000 on validation to avoid spending $30,000 on a product nobody wants.
- **Define 5-7 core features maximum**: Every feature adds time and cost. Start with the minimum, add more after launch.
- **Budget 20% for post-launch**: Plan for ongoing costs: hosting, maintenance, support. MVP is just the beginning.
- **Get fixed-price quotes**: Hourly rates can spiral. Insist on detailed scope and fixed-price proposals.
- **Account for hidden costs**: Add 20-30% buffer for subscriptions, legal, and unexpected expenses.

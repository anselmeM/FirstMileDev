Don't get blindsided by hidden costs. Here's the complete budget breakdown for every phase of MVP development.

Hidden Costs Most Founders Forget

These costs don't show up in development quotes but can derail your budget:

Hosting & Infrastructure ($50-500/month)

AWS, Heroku, Vercel costs add up quickly at scale. MVP hosting starts at $50/month but can hit $500+ with traffic.

Software Subscriptions ($50-300/month)

Email (Mailchimp/SendGrid), analytics (Mixpanel), payments (Stripe), SMS (Twilio)—costs accumulate.

Post-Launch Support ($500-2,000/month)

Bugs, feature requests, security updates. Budget 20% of development cost annually for maintenance.

Legal & Compliance ($500-5,000+)

Terms of service, privacy policy, possibly HIPAA/SOC2 for regulated industries.

How to Budget Wisely

Before you start spending, follow this checklist:

  • Validate before building

    Spend $2,000 on validation to avoid spending $30,000 on a product nobody wants.

  • Define 5-7 core features maximum

    Every feature adds time and cost. Start with the minimum, add more after launch.

  • Budget 20% for post-launch

    Plan for ongoing costs: hosting, maintenance, support. MVP is just the beginning.

  • Get fixed-price quotes

    Hourly rates can spiral. Insist on detailed scope and fixed-price proposals.

  • Account for hidden costs

    Add 20-30% buffer for subscriptions, legal, and unexpected expenses.

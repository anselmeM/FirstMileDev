The Challenge

Mike, an aspiring entrepreneur, had an idea for a niche sneaker marketplace focused on hard-to-find retro basketball shoes. He was ready to spend his life savings - $50,000 - on development without validating whether anyone would actually buy.

He approached FirstMileDev hoping to build the full marketplace. Instead, we recommended a validation-first approach - test the market demand before writing a single line of code.

The Solution

Week 1: Landing Page + Ad Testing

We created a high-converting landing page showcasing the concept, then ran targeted Facebook and Instagram ads to sneaker enthusiasts. The ads highlighted the unique value proposition: "Hard-to-find retro basketball shoes, curated for collectors."

Week 2: Data Analysis

Analyzed the campaign data. Key findings: High ad impressions but low click-through rate (0.8%), and of those who clicked, only 12% entered their email for updates. The cost per interested lead was $47 - indicating weak demand.

The Verdict

We presented Mike with our findings: The target market was too small to support a standalone business. Instead of building and hoping for success, we recommended he test the concept as a side project or focus on a broader market segment.
